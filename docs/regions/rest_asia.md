# Rest of Asia

R10 macro-region covering South-East Asia and the Pacific island states.
Common composition: Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines, Singapore,
Thailand, Vietnam, Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar, Brunei, Timor-Leste, Mongolia,
plus various Pacific island groupings (see Extended Data Table 1 in the
manuscript for the exact composition). Pakistan and Bangladesh sit in the
India macro-region rather than here under standard IPCC R10 conventions.

## Physical setting (brief)

Rest of Asia is dominantly cooling-driven and ITCZ-influenced. The South-East
Asian monsoon and the position of the Inter-Tropical Convergence Zone drive
strong seasonal patterns in both solar (cloud cover during the wet monsoon)
and wind. Demand is dominantly diurnal (cooling load tracks daytime
temperatures); seasonal demand shares are modest (~15–25% across most
member countries). Mongolia is a continental-interior outlier in the
otherwise tropical/maritime ensemble. Solar–demand seasonal alignment is
weakly positive for most members; wind–demand seasonal alignment is mixed.
