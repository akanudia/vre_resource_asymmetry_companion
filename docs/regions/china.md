R10 macro-region covering mainland China plus typically associated entities

## Physical setting (brief)

China is the exception within the heating-driven Northern Hemisphere group.
Most heating-driven temperate regions (Europe, Reforming Economies, North
America) sit in the upper-left quadrant of the Fig 4b alignment landscape:
positive solar–demand alignment via winter heating peaks (negative) and
positive wind–demand alignment via winter storm tracks. **China sits in the
lower-left** because the East-Asian monsoon damps mid-latitude winter wind,
weakening or flipping the wind–demand seasonal correlation that the rest of
the heating group enjoys. Within-region heterogeneity is large: northern
China is heating-driven with continental winters; southern China is
cooling-driven with subtropical summers.
