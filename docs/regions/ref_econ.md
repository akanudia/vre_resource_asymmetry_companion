# Reforming Economies

R10 macro-region covering Russia, the European and Central Asian former
Soviet Union states, and (per AR6 WGIII) typically Turkey (see Extended Data
Table 1 in the manuscript for the exact composition).

## Physical setting (brief)

The Reforming Economies macro-region is heavily heating-driven. Northern
member countries (Russia, Norway-equivalent latitudes, the Caucasus interior)
have seasonal demand shares in the 50–55% range; solar seasonal share is
elevated (~22% in Norway / Finland / Sweden, similar pattern in Russia) due
to the high-latitude winter solar minimum. Wind seasonal alignment with
demand is positive (winter storm-track wind coincides with winter heating).
Within-country resource heterogeneity is large for the geographically
extensive members (Russia has ~130 cluster cells in the variance-decomposition
setup); supply-curve refinement therefore has a wide active-deployment range.
