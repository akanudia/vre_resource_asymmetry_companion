# North America

R10 macro-region covering the USA, Canada, and Mexico (USA and Canada
modelled individually in R70; Mexico typically appears in Latin America for
some IPCC R10 definitions — see Extended Data Table 1 in the manuscript for
the exact composition used here).

## Physical setting (brief)

North America spans roughly 20°N to 80°N and combines climate signatures:
the contiguous USA is mixed heating/cooling by region (heating-dominated in
the Northeast and Upper Midwest; cooling-dominated in the South and
Southwest); Canada is heating-driven; Mexico is cooling-driven. Aggregated
to the R10 macro-region, the wind–demand seasonal alignment is positive
(winter storm-track wind in continental interior). The USA is one of the
largest countries in terms of within-country resource heterogeneity for wind
(~130 cluster cells in the variance-decomposition setup), so refining the
supply-curve representation has large effects on which tranches of resource
get exposed to the optimiser.
