# Middle East

R10 macro-region covering the Arabian Peninsula, the Levant, Iraq, Iran, and
typically Egypt (see Extended Data Table 1 in the manuscript for the exact
composition).

## Physical setting (brief)

The Middle East is the most strongly cooling-driven macro-region in the
study. Saudi Arabia has the highest seasonal demand share among the 62 R70
countries at **84%**; Oman 82%, UAE 68%, Iraq 63%. Solar–demand seasonal
alignment is **strongly positive** (summer solar peak coincides with summer
cooling demand). Offshore wind in some Middle East coastal zones is highly
seasonal — Oman reaches the highest country-tech seasonal share of any
country in the study (~63%) — but onshore wind variance composition is
dominated by stochastic synoptic + day-to-day weather. The macro-region's
position in the Fig 4b alignment landscape: lower-right (positive solar
seasonal alignment, weak/negative wind seasonal alignment).
