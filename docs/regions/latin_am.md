# Latin America

R10 macro-region covering Mexico, Central America, the Caribbean, and South
America. See Extended Data Table 1 in the manuscript for the exact composition.

## Physical setting (brief)

Latin America is dominated by trade-wind / ITCZ migration on the Pacific
coast: Venezuela, Colombia, Ecuador have seasonal wind shares of 35–46%,
among the highest globally. Most of the macro-region is cooling-dominated.
Solar–demand seasonal alignment is weakly positive. Brazil shows extreme
within-country resource heterogeneity for wind (CF range 9–35%, ~4×) and
moderate for solar (16–23%), so refining supply curves has large effects on
wind allocation. The macro-region spans both hemispheres, which complicates
the seasonal alignment story relative to single-hemisphere macroregions.
